Terms And Definitions

Here are some simple football terms and definitions to consider when learning about not only your position, but football in general.

Touchdown(TD)
When a player carries the ball across the opponent's goal line or catches a pass in the end zone, resulting in 6 points.
Field Goal(FG)
A scoring play where a kicker attempts to kick the ball through the opponent's goalposts, worth 3 points.
Extra Point(PAT)
An attempt to kick the ball through the opponent's goalposts after a touchdown, worth 1 point.
Two-Point Conversion
An attempt to score 2 points after a touchdown by running or passing the ball into the end zone instead of kicking for an extra point.
Offense
The team with possession of the ball, trying to advance it down the field to score.
Defense
The team trying to prevent the offense from scoring by tackling players, intercepting passes, or causing fumbles.
Blitz
A defensive strategy where additional players rush the quarterback in an attempt to disrupt the play.
Interception(INT)
When a defensive player catches a pass intended for an offensive player.
Fumble
When a player loses possession of the ball, often due to being tackled.
Sack
When a defensive player tackles the quarterback behind the line of scrimmage before they can throw a pass.
